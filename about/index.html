What is Design Engineering?

"Design Engineering" means a lot of different things to a lot of different people, even within my own degree program, and that ambiguity is intentional.

At Mines, DE is built around the idea that real problems don't fit neatly into a single discipline. The program combines a rigorous foundation in engineering fundamentals with a flexible selection of technical electives and a sequence of project-based design studios. Rather than prescribing a narrow path, DE allows students to shape an education around their interests while continuously applying what they learn.

My focus is in robotics and automation. I've deliberately balanced my coursework across MechE, EE, and CS because effective robotic systems demand all three. This interdisciplinary approach has given me a big picture understanding of how complex systems are designed, built, and integrated from hardware and control to software and user interaction.

The design studios bring all of this together. Through open-ended, real world projects, I've been able to test my technical skills while also engaging with systems modeling, stakeholder needs, and the broader context surrounding a design. For me, design engineering isn't just about making things work, it's about making them work for people.
